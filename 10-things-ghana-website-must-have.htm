10 Things Your Ghana Business Website Must Have in 2026

Samuel AHIABA
Founder · SamzyDigital
October 2025 6 min read
Back to Blog

You've finally decided to build a website for your Ghanaian business. Congratulations – that's a huge step. But a website isn't just a digital brochure. It's your most powerful sales tool, working 24/7 to attract, convert, and retain customers.

To do that, it needs more than just a nice design. Here are the 10 non‑negotiable elements every Ghana business website must have in 2026.

THE CHECKLIST

Print this list and check your current site against it. If you're missing more than three items, you're leaving money on the table.

1. Mobile‑First Design

Over 80% of Ghanaians browse the internet on their phones. If your site isn't easy to navigate on a small screen, you've already lost them. A mobile‑first design means everything is optimised for phones first, then scales up to larger screens. This includes readable text, touch‑friendly buttons, and fast loading times.

2. Clear Call‑to‑Action (CTA)

Every page should have a clear next step. "Call Now", "Book Appointment", "Get a Quote", "Order Now" – whatever action you want visitors to take, make it obvious and easy. Use contrasting colours and place CTAs above the fold.

3. MTN MoMo & Card Payment Integration

If you're selling anything online, you must accept Mobile Money. Ghanaians trust MoMo. Integrate it via Hubtel, Paystack, or directly. Even if you're a service business, allowing deposits via MoMo reduces no‑shows and builds trust.

4. WhatsApp Integration

Ghana's favourite communication channel is WhatsApp. Add a WhatsApp chat button that opens a pre‑filled message to your business number. It's the easiest way for customers to ask questions and for you to close sales.

5. Fast Loading Speed

Ghana's internet speeds are improving, but you can't afford a slow site. Aim for under 3 seconds load time. Optimise images, use a good hosting provider, and minimise scripts. Google also uses speed as a ranking factor.

6. SEO Foundations

Your site should be optimised to be found on Google. This means proper page titles, meta descriptions, heading structure, alt text for images, and a sitemap. Also, claim your Google Business Profile and make sure your address, phone, and hours are consistent across the web.

7. Trust Signals: Reviews & Testimonials

Display real reviews from past customers. Use Google reviews, or a simple testimonial section. Include names and photos where possible. Trust builds confidence, and confidence converts.

8. Easy Contact Information

Never hide your phone number or email. Place them prominently in the header or footer. A contact page with a form is good, but also provide a click‑to‑call link for mobile users.

9. Location Information (for local businesses)

If you have a physical location, embed a Google Map with your exact address. Also mention neighbourhood and landmarks – e.g., "Opposite Kanda Mall". This helps customers find you and boosts local SEO.

10. Analytics & Tracking

How will you know if your site is working? Install Google Analytics (or a privacy‑friendly alternative) to see where visitors come from, what they do, and where they drop off. This data is gold for improving your site over time.

Bonus: Ongoing Support & Maintenance

A website isn't a one‑and‑done project. Technology changes, security updates happen, and content needs refreshing. Make sure you have a plan for regular updates. At SamzyDigital, we include a 30‑day support period and offer affordable maintenance packages.

Is Your Site Missing These?

If you read through this list and realised your current site is falling short, don't worry – you're not alone. Many Ghanaian businesses started with a basic site that worked at the time, but the digital landscape has evolved.
